# Career Story

This document describes the recurring themes throughout Veerakran Sereerungruangkul's career.

Unlike `career.md`, this document is not a timeline.

Its purpose is to explain the kind of engineer Veerakran is and provide context that AI can use when writing About pages, portfolio content, interview preparation, and future resumes.

---

# Overview

My career has never focused on a single framework, programming language, or industry.

Instead, it has consistently centered around understanding unfamiliar technologies, integrating different systems, and delivering practical software that works in production.

Although the projects themselves have been very different, the underlying engineering problems have been surprisingly similar.

---

# Early Career

I started my career developing enterprise software for government agencies, public organizations, and private-sector clients.

The work ranged from traditional web applications to Windows desktop software, hardware integration, internal information systems, and public-facing services.

Because my company developed software for many different clients, every project introduced different technical requirements and unfamiliar technologies.

This environment taught me to become comfortable reading documentation, researching solutions, and adapting quickly to new domains.

---

# Becoming the "Research Guy"

One recurring pattern throughout my career is being assigned work that required researching technologies the team had not used before.

Most of the time I was not responsible for choosing the technology.

Instead, my responsibility was understanding how it worked, integrating it into existing systems, solving implementation problems, and making it reliable enough for production.

This happened repeatedly across cloud platforms, multiplayer services, payment systems, hardware devices, mobile SDKs, desktop software, and government integrations.

Looking back, this has become the defining characteristic of my career.

---

# Transition to Live-Service Games

Joining Hengtech changed the industry I worked in but not the type of engineering problems I enjoyed solving.

The technologies became different, but the work remained centered around integration.

Instead of connecting enterprise systems and hardware, I was integrating multiplayer platforms, cloud services, payment systems, voice communication, advertising SDKs, and backend infrastructure for live-service games.

The same curiosity and problem-solving approach continued to apply.

---

# What I Enjoy

The projects I enjoy most are those where different systems need to work together reliably.

Examples include:

- Backend APIs
- Cloud services
- Third-party integrations
- Hardware integration
- Multiplayer services
- Payment systems
- Enterprise workflows

I enjoy understanding how each system works individually and then designing software that connects them together.

---

# How I Approach Technology

I rarely evaluate technologies based on popularity.

Most technologies I have used were selected because they fit a project's requirements or because they were chosen by technical leadership.

My focus has always been learning how to use those technologies effectively rather than becoming attached to a specific stack.

As a result, I am comfortable moving between different languages, frameworks, cloud services, and platforms as projects require.

---

# Engineering Philosophy

I prefer practical software over clever software.

Good software should be:

- Maintainable
- Reliable
- Easy to understand
- Appropriate for the project's requirements

I value long-term maintainability more than unnecessary complexity.

---

# AI-assisted Development

AI has become part of my daily development workflow.

I use it to accelerate implementation, debugging, refactoring, documentation, and research.

However, AI is a tool rather than a replacement for engineering judgement.

I still consider understanding the underlying technology to be essential.

---

# Looking Forward

I want to continue working as a Backend Developer on projects involving platform development, systems integration, cloud services, and production software.

Rather than chasing a specific technology, I am interested in solving meaningful engineering problems and continuing to learn through practical software development.
